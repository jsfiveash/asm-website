Frequently Asked Questions

What are the goals of ASM?

The goals of the ASM are:

  • Discovery, investigation, and conservation of Maryland's archeological resources.
  • Proper recording of archeological sites.
  • Respect for the fragile and non-renewable nature of archeological sites.
  • Creation of bonds between avocational and professional archeologists.
  • Development of opportunities for working with professional archeologists through directed fieldwork, analysis, and reporting.
  • Development of opportunities for working with professional archeologists through directed fieldwork, analysis, and reporting.
  • Public dissemination of information about Maryland's buried heritage.
  • Publication of the results of all excavations undertaken by the Society.

Why should I become a Member of ASM?

Among the immediate benefits of ASM membership, you will:

  • Learn about upcoming events and issues from ASMInk, a monthly newsletter.
  • Receive publication notices for the biannual journal Maryland Archeology, detailing exciting discoveries.
  • Attend Society events at reduced admission.

You may also participate in numerous activities and events throughout the year, including:

  • The Annual Field Session in Maryland Archeology, a yearly, professionally directed, excavation on a Maryland archeological site.
  • The Annual Fall Meeting and the Annual Spring Symposium.
  • The Annual Workshop in Archeology.
  • Ongoing, professionally directed, volunteer activities including excavations, processing and cataloging artifacts, and other laboratory tasks.
  • Local Chapter meetings and sponsored events.
  • A wide variety of lectures, displays, and hands-on activities during the statewide Maryland Archeology Month celebration.
Who may participate in the annual Tyler Bastian Field Session?

Anyone intersted in archeology may participate in the field Session. Requirements for participation are:

  • Register for the days that you wish to participate. Registration is available on-line, prior to the session, however, participants may register at the site.
  • Complete a liability waiver provided at the site. The waiver states that participants will not hold ASM, MHT or the owner of the property liable for injuries incurred during the work, that photos taken by ASM and MHT may be used in publications and that the participant agrees to the Statement of Ethics that cover all ASM activities.
  • Agree to follow directions of the principal investigator, professional staff and ASM leadership while on the site.
Can chidren participate in the ASM Field Session?

In general, children are welcome to participate in the field session with some limitations:

  • Participants under 18 must be accompanied by a parent or guardian who is registered to participate.
  • The field site is, essentially, an industrial work site. Participants must understand and abide by the health and safety rules that are briefed at the beginning of each day.
  • The field session is a scientific endeavour in which standards and procedures established by the principal investigator must be followed.
  • Participants who can't, or won't abide by safety, procedural or ethical standards will be asked to leave the site.
